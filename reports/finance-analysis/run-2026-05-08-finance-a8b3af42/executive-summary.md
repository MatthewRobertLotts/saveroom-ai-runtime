## Executive read
SaveRoom is generating meaningful order volume, but the commercial model is still being held back by low-ticket sales. The core issue is not demand, it is basket size. Revenue is concentrated in a few clear winners, while a long tail of low-priced products is absorbing a disproportionate share of fees.

The strongest commercial drivers in this dataset are:
- **Mega Dream Japanese**
- **Phantasmal Flames Booster Pack English**
- **Perfect Order Booster Pack English**
- **Nihil Zero Japanese**
- **Battle Partners Booster Pack Japanese**

These lines combine repeatability with real contribution to net revenue. By contrast, very low-AOV pack lines and low-value accessories are materially fee inefficient.

## Priority actions
1. Push more bundle logic around the strongest Japanese and English winners.
2. Reduce reliance on sub-£3 single-pack outcomes where fee drag becomes severe.
3. Move weak low-AOV inventory into bundles, ladders, or website-led value packs.
4. Treat giveaways and ultra-low priced support lines as intentional engagement spend, not revenue products.
5. Track contribution after fees by product family weekly, not just gross sales.
