# Profitability Analysis

## Core profitability view
This dataset only supports analysis **after marketplace and payment fees**, not full landed margin, because product cost, shipping cost, labor, packaging, and breakage are not present. Even with that limitation, the picture is clear.

- Net after fees: **£10,139.47**
- Revenue retained after fees: **86.47%**
- Revenue lost to fees before COGS and fulfillment: **13.53%**

## Strongest contributors after fees
- **Mega Dream Japanese**: net after fees **£1,633.48** from **212** paid orders
- **Phantasmal Flames Booster Pack English**: **£1,614.05** from **235** paid orders
- **Perfect Order Booster Pack English**: **£985.27** from **196** paid orders
- **Nihil Zero Japanese**: **£524.48** from **149** paid orders
- **Battle Partners Booster Pack Japanese**: **£486.16** from **178** paid orders

## Weakest commercial patterns
The weakest inventory is not necessarily low total revenue only. The real warning sign is **low selling price plus repeat volume**, because that creates recurring fee leakage.

Examples:
- **Future Flash Booster Pack**: 20 orders, AOV **£1.20**, net per order about **£0.82** before any product or shipping cost
- **Dynamax Clash Thunder CS1a Booster Pack**: 29 orders, AOV **£1.90**, net per order about **£1.46** before COGS
- **Vivid Portrayals Obsidian CS2a Booster Pack**: 30 orders, AOV **£2.50**, net per order about **£2.01** before COGS
- **Glory of Team Rocket Booster Pack**: 45 orders, AOV **£2.55**, fee rate **19.78%**

## Profitability conclusion
SaveRoom has enough demand to scale, but profits will stay compressed unless low-ticket conversion gets consolidated into larger baskets. The best improvement lever is not squeezing customers harder. It is getting more packs into each paid order.
